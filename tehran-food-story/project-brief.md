# Project Brief

## 1. Final Topic

**Working title:** *When Staples Slip Away: Tehran's Food Shock, 2025-2026*

## 2. Core Angle

This project should not try to explain all of Iran's inflation at once, and it should not stay at the broad level of "food prices went up." The sharper version of the story is:

**How much did staple food prices rise in Tehran between spring 2025 and spring 2026, and how did surging rice and wheat flour prices reshape the cost of everyday life for ordinary households?**

This angle works well because it gives the project:

- a clear place: Tehran
- a clear time window: 2025-2026
- a clear object of study: staple foods and essential household items
- a clear audience: the general public
- a natural bridge between market prices and official food inflation data

## 3. Why Food Instead of Rent

- Tehran food data is easier to support with continuous public evidence, especially through FAO/GIEWS market reporting.
- Food categories are easier to split into comparison views and linked interactions.
- The story can begin with rice and wheat flour, then expand to oils, dairy, fruit, and vegetables.
- The final result is more naturally public-facing and narrative-driven than a narrowly real-estate-oriented story.

## 4. Narrative Arc

### Opening

Begin with a simple reader-facing question:

**If you went shopping for basic staples in Tehran in spring 2025 and then again in spring 2026, what would you see?**

### Section One

Show the market-level staple signals in Tehran:

- rice prices accelerating rapidly
- wheat flour climbing under subsidy shifts and currency pressure
- price levels remaining extremely high in early 2026

### Section Two

Expand from market goods to official food-category inflation:

- bread and cereals
- meat
- dairy
- oils and fats
- fruits and nuts
- vegetables

### Section Three

Explain why this is not only a price story but a cost-of-living story:

- food inflation moved faster than overall inflation
- several key categories crossed into triple-digit year-over-year increases
- household purchasing power was visibly squeezed

### Ending

Return to the household level:

- which goods became unaffordable first
- which pressures look temporary and which look structural
- what this says about what ordinary consumers in Tehran are being asked to absorb

## 6. Confirmed Starter Evidence

- In February 2026, wheat flour in the Tehran market was around `520,000 IRR/kg`.
- In February 2026, wheat flour in the Tehran market was about `200%` higher year over year.
- In February 2026, rice in the Tehran market was roughly `4x` its year-earlier level.
- In March 2026, Iran's `food, beverages, and tobacco` inflation was about `112.5%` year over year.
- In March 2026, `oils and fats` was about `219%` year over year.
- In March 2026, `bread and cereals` was about `140%` year over year.
