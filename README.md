# Tehran Food Story

This folder contains a static data-driven narrative project on food affordability in Tehran.

## Current project angle

**Working title:** *When Staples Slip Away: Tehran's Food Shock, 2025-2026*

**Core question:** How sharply did staple food prices rise in Tehran between spring 2025 and spring 2026, and what does that reveal about household affordability?

Instead of trying to explain every part of Iran's inflation story at once, this project stays focused on a public-facing question:

- what happened to staple foods in Tehran
- how quickly prices moved
- which categories were hit hardest
- why the jump mattered for ordinary households

## What is included

- `project-brief.md`: English planning notes for the assignment

## Suggested narrative arc

1. Open with a human question about what a basic shopping trip costs now.
2. Anchor the story in Tehran market evidence for rice and wheat flour.
3. Expand outward with official food-category inflation to show that the shock is not limited to one commodity.
4. Close with affordability: food inflation versus overall inflation, wages, and household pressure.
